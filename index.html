  • Deforestation is defined as cutting down forests to clear the land, for the making of materials, or for other use

    • Why Deforestation is Harmful:

    Deforestation can harm us in factors of climate. In the World Wild Life website it talks about how Forests are a vital role in mitigating climate change because they act as a carbon sink—soaking up carbon dioxide that would otherwise be free in the atmosphere and contribute to ongoing changes in climate patterns. Therefore, by elimating the amount of trees we are increasing the amount of carbon dioxide we leave in the planet and that can make a negative impact to our atomosphere which will increase climate change.

    Deforestations Affects on Global Warming

    Deforestation increases global warming drastically. In the website The London School of Ecomnomic and Political Science it talks about how in the last decade, the largest amounts of deforestation occurred across the humid tropics. The UN Food and Agriculture Organisation estimates that around 129 million hectares of forest – almost equivalent to the area of South Africa – were lost between 1990 and 2015.

    Deforestation Affects on Animals

    Deforestation takes away the homes of poor animals who are just trying to live. It not only takes their homes but it can leave them open to predatory species that they won't usually encounter if not to deforestation. These animals are put into situations they shouldn't be put in because humans are cutting down their homes.
